Unknown Cat Facts

  • Cats use their whiskers not only for navigation but also to measure the width of openings. When a cat's whiskers touch a surface, it sends signals to their brain about the width of the space. However, too much whisker stimulation can cause whisker fatigue, and some cats might become stressed in tight spaces.
  • Cats have a special organ called Jacobson's organ or the vomeronasal organ located on the roof of their mouth. This organ detects pheromones, chemical signals that animals use for communication. When a cat curls back its lips and opens its mouth after sniffing something, it's called the Flehmen response, and it helps the cat analyze scents more effectively.
  • While it's widely known that cats purr when they're content, it's interesting to note that the frequency of a domestic cat's purring falls within a range that is known to be medically therapeutic. The vibrations created by purring may have a calming effect on humans and can potentially promote healing of bones and tissues.
